About

Human history is marked by discovery of new materials. First we had the stone age, then the bronze age, and now we are in the silicon age. The NanoML team at Purdue University is bringing about the quantum age with the assistance of bleeding edge machine learning algorithms. The group is composed of undergraduate, graduate, and postdoctoral fellows of the Purdue NanoMeta and Purdue Center for Quantum Science and Engineering Institute (PQSEI). In collaboration with PQSEI, the Elmore Center for Emerging Frontiers Center and the Quantum Science Center at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, the NanoML group is dedicated to bringing about the next generation of quantum and AI algorithms for the study of new pysics. We take a first principles approach to machine learning and quantum computing. Starting from the foundational mathematics of statistical learning, generative modeling, and theoretical physics, we build new approaches by having a solid understanding of the mathematics and physics behind the algorithms.

Our group is currently working on the following projects:

  • Quantum Optimization for Inverse Design
  • Quantum Generative Modeling
  • Generative Modeling for Material Design
  • Diffusion Models for Statistical Physics
  • Computer Vision for Material Characterization
